The problem

About 30% of city traffic is caused by people looking for a parking spot. Trying to inform them better, cities all over Flanders publish real-time data about parking availability but each in their own difficult to integrate format.

The solution: linked open data

A best practice for exposing, sharing, and connecting pieces of data, information, and knowledge on the Semantic Web using URIs and RDF.

Real-time + historic

By keeping historical data we can not only analyse the past but also predict the future. Real-time data also enables users to observe the data they need.

Cost-efficient

No need for expensive servers, linked open data is kept in files that are processed by the browser. This keeps infrastructural costs low and allows for easy maintenance.

Flexible

Our solution is made as generic as possible to make sure that added data from other sources is as easy as possible.

How

The road to our solution.

  • The format mess

    To solve this urban problem of parking spaces we use linked open data. As of this moment parking companies and cities don’t publish their data in the same format. They all use a different format. To address this problem we choose implemented linked open data fragments.

  • The gathering

    We gathered all the parking data from different companies and cities and centralised them. We made sure they were all in the same format, linked open data, and started to keep the data. This way we have historical data that’s easily accessible.

  • Usage

    To prove our point we created a front-end in angular 4 the illustrate the power of linked open data.
